Role of Credit Relationship Management

  • Manage client relationships throughout the project lifecycle from submitting the application to the signing
  • Collect, validate, and organize client information and required documentation
  • Coordinate project evaluation with internal stakeholders (PSD, MCD, Credit Risk, Legal, and other related teams)
  • Conduct credit evaluation of the client and the project:
    • Review financial statements (Income Statement, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow)
    • Assess liquidity, leverage, profitability, and cash generation
  • Prepare: Ownership Report and Project Evaluation Report (PER)

Working Capital ‚Äî Appraisal & Examples

For working capital financing, the RM officer is responsible for preparing the appraisal, which focuses primarily on the company‚Äôs financial analysis, liquidity, leverage, and repayment capacity. The appraisal includes following:

1) Project Evaluation Report (PER)

  • Ownership structure and shareholders
  • Background and rationale
  • Financial analysis usually for the three years (current year and last two years)
  • Model results summary
  • Loan section
  • Credit check and SIDF relationship
  • Final recommendation of the project
üìé PER example (GulfPack WC report)

2) Financial Model

  • Inputting the financial statements of the actual years
  • Insert the proper projection for the upcoming years
  • The model auto calculate the working capital need, repayment capacity and related ratios (Current ratio, Leverage ratio, DSCR)

Project Finance ‚Äî Appraisal & Examples

For project finance, the RM officer is responsible for preparing the appraisal, which focuses primarily on the project‚Äôs feasibility, sponsor strength, long-term cash-flow generation, and repayment capacity. The appraisal includes the following:

1) Ownership Assessment Report

  • Ownership structure, shareholders and company‚Äôs management structure
  • Financial analysis for the borrower and related parties
  • Net worth assessment
  • Credit check and SIDF relationship

2) Project Evaluation Report

  • Project background, scope and rationale
  • Ownership assessment
  • Products and capacities
  • Project implementation status
  • Market Section
  • Financial analysis summary
  • Project costs and sources of finance
  • Loan section
  • Final recommendation

3) Financial model

For project finance loan, RM develops two financial perspectives: a standalone model to assess the project itself and a consolidated model to assess the sponsor‚Äôs overall financial strength.

Standalone vs Consolidated

Key differences between both approaches:

Aspect Standalone Consolidated
Scope Evaluates the project itself as an independent unit Evaluates the project together with the sponsor‚Äôs existing business
Purpose Assess the financial viability and repayment capacity of the project alone Assess the overall credit strength and support capacity of the whole company
Financial Inputs Expected project‚Äôs revenue (volumes & selling prices) provided by MSD; project costs (raw material, labor, depreciation and amortization) provided by PSD Historical financial statements of the entire company and the subject project
SIDF Use Determines whether the project can service its own debt (project viability) Determines whether the group can fulfill the obligation, especially during operating period

What does SIDF offer?

SIDF offers a diverse range of short, medium, and long-term financial solutions, specially designed to meet the needs of industrial projects. These solutions include working capital financing, supply chain financing, project financing, acquisition financing, and multipurpose financing. üéØ Main idea: SIDF financing is structured to support industrial growth‚Äîwhether it‚Äôs day-to-day operations, expansion, or strategic investment.

Key financing solutions

üíº
Working Capital

Supports day-to-day operations such as raw materials, payroll, and operating needs.

üîó
Supply Chain Financing

Helps businesses manage supply and supplier payments more efficiently.

üèóÔ∏è
Project Financing

Finances new projects or expansions that increase rated production capacity.

üß©
Multipurpose Financing

Funds upgrades that improve efficiency without increasing rated capacity.

ü§ù
Acquisition Financing

Supports strategic acquisitions that enhance capabilities and value creation.
